Alternative Pathways

A Low MCAT Score Is
Not the End of Your Story.

Many of the medical programs we offer do not require the MCAT at all — or place far less weight on it than traditional Canadian and American schools. Your score does not define your future as a physician.

Clearing the Confusion

Myths vs. Facts

Myth

"A low MCAT means I can never become a doctor."

Many accredited international medical programs do not require the MCAT, and their graduates go on to practise medicine in Canada and the U.S. after completing licensing exams.

Fact

Many pathways don't require the MCAT at all.

Programs like the 5-Year and 6-Year BSc/MD and the Pre-Medical Master's are evaluated on your science grades, motivation, and overall profile — not your MCAT score.

Myth

"International medical degrees aren't recognized in Canada."

Licensing is based on your training quality and performance on Canadian licensing exams — not where you attended medical school.

Fact

Accredited programs open doors to North American practice.

Graduates of the programs we advise on have successfully completed USMLE and MCCQE exams and are practising medicine in Canada and the U.S.
